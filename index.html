FootprintFree: CO2 Emission Calculator

Vehicle emissions, including carbon dioxide (CO2) and other greenhouse gases, released during the burning of fossil fuels like gasoline or diesel, pose environmental concerns. These emissions contribute to air pollution and can have adverse effects on air quality and human health. Reducing carbon emissions from vehicles is imperative in mitigating these negative impacts.

The FootprintFree calculator determines the carbon emission of different vehicles based on the distance driven. Utilize the Google Maps widget to select two destinations and quickly determine the distance in miles. The app also calculates the carbon emissions based on the distance and your vehicle's miles per gallon (MPG). Additionally, it shows you how many trees could be saved by walking instead of driving.

Try it now and start making a positive impact on the planet!

About the Programmers

The FootprintFree creators, Joshua Santy and Simon Edmunds, who are both talented developers, teamed up for the 2023 Missouri S&T Hackathon Event called "Pickhacks." With this year's theme of "Environment" and 39 hours on the clock, and Josh and Simon sought to create an innovative web app that could calculate carbon emissions using Angular Google Maps API. Due to the steep, and I mean steep, learning curve of AngularJS, the duo wisely opted to compile their code in Replit without using Angular. Their app, aptly named 'FootprintFree', provides users with an easy-to-use tool to estimate the CO2 emissions of their travel routes. By simply clicking the starting and ending destinations, the app calculates the distance in miles, factors in a vehicle's miles per gallon (MPG) provided by the user, and presents the estimated carbon emissions. The Footprint Free app can help raise awareness about carbon emissions from transportation and promote sustainable travel choices.
